Product update · Setup

Seasons & Teams

Season-ticket data now belongs to a season and a team, not to one overwritable status field — so a new year's roster import adds to a fan's history instead of erasing last year's.

Area: Setup → Seasons & teams
Status: In prototype
Related: STH entitlements

01The problem this solves

Season-ticket holder groups reset every year, and a shared venue can run more than one team's list at once — e.g. United Center's Bulls roster and Blackhawks roster are entirely separate, each with their own tiers.

Today that's handled as one flat, overwritable tag on a fan. Re-importing this year's CSV risks erasing last year's — and there was no clean place to hold four-plus concurrent groupings (two teams × multiple years × tiers) without the UI multiplying tabs indefinitely.

— raised in the Jul 21 product review

02What's new

Venue & team registryfoundation
A venue can host more than one team. Each team keeps its own seasons, tiers, and rosters — nothing about the Bulls' setup touches the Blackhawks'.
Season history, not a statuscore change
Every enrollment is scoped to a season. Importing this year's roster appends a new record — it never overwrites a prior season's data.
Per-season tiers & benefitsconfigurable
Plan codes, tier names, and benefits (e.g. "20% off all F&B") are defined per season, so pricing or benefits can change year over year without rewriting history.
Renewal window trackingper season
Open/close dates live on the season record itself — this is what a renewal-nudge campaign reads to know when to fire.
Import match rulesper season
Each season declares its match key — account number where the source system provides one, email/phone as the fallback — so this year's row lands on the correct existing fan.
Renewal outcome, on importnew signal
Every roster import reports who renewed, upgraded, downgraded, is brand-new, or didn't come back — a real retention number, not just a headcount.
Explicit "current season"one switch
One flag decides what "current" means everywhere else — loyalty, promotions, campaign audiences all just read it, with no need to understand the history underneath.
